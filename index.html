Samson Koelle

I got a PhD in statistics from the University of Washington advised by Marina Meila. My thesis research was on algorithms for parametric dimension reduction that give interpretability to learned representations. This work applies techniques from optimization, machine learning, differential geometry, and high-dimensional statistics to quantum chemistry. In my research career I was also working on neuro with Stefan Mihalas at Allen Brain, stem cell with Cindy Dunbar at the National Institutes of Health, immunology with Raphael Gottardo at Fred Hutch, and supply chain at Amazon. I have a BA in Math from Columbia University. I am now a founder of Uberduck!
